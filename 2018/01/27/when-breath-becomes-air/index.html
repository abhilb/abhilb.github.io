[Book review] When breath becomes air

For the past couple of years, I have tried to read at least a couple of memoirs a year. I am glad that this year, I chose this book. This is a memoir of a neurosurgeon, Paul Kalanithi who was diagnosed with lung cancer. He passed away in 2015 and this book was published posthumously.

In the first part of the memoir, the author narrates about his life prior to the cancer diagnosis; his childhood, how his mother got him into reading classics and poems. He then mentions about his studies, about philosophy and various philosophers who have influenced him, about his journey of becoming a neurosurgeon. The section where he tells us about the gruelling and stressfull work schedule of a doctor, the tough decisions one has to take as a doctor and how many doctors fail to cope up with such pressure was totally new to me. I knew that Doctors have a tough life, but it paints a whole different picture when you look through the eyes of the author.

In the latter part of the memoir, which by the way is very emotional and inspiring, the author takes us through the different stages of the cancer treatment. The life of the patient and that of his loved ones and the family gets caught up in a whirlwind. Everyone will die someday but to know that you are going to die soon makes everything different. It is so scary, isnt’t it? All your dreams and aspirations are shattered. But it is truely commendable how the author had taken up this challenge. Not only did he finish this book, he and his wife had a baby during this period, he went back to work for a brief period of time.

I really really liked this one. My rating 5/5.
